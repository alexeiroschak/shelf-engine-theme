Shelfbot Co. DBA Shelf Engine Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 15, 2019

Welcome to the website (the “Site”) of Shelfbot Co. dba Shelf Engine (“Company,” “we,” “us,” or “our”). Company provides services related to the food and beverage industry, which includes, but is not limited to a platform designed to provide retailers (and any retailer authorized individuals) and vendors or commissaries a web-based order prediction engine which analyzes historical order and sales data and makes purchase recommendations for retailers for vendor or commissary goods and services (collectively, including the Site and any related mobile applications, the “Service”).

This Privacy Policy explains what Personal Data (defined below) we collect, how we use and share that data, and your choices concerning our data practices.

Before using the Service or submitting any Personal Data to Company, please review this Privacy Policy carefully and contact us at privacy@shelfengine.com if you have any questions. By using the Service, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree to this Privacy Policy, please do not access the Site or otherwise use the Service.

Cookies: Please see the “Cookies” section below to learn more about how we use cookies.

Cookies: We use cookies to operate and administer our Site, gather usage data on our Site and improve your experience on it. A “cookie” is a piece of information sent to your browser by a website you visit. Cookies can be stored on your computer for different periods of time. Some cookies expire after a certain amount of time, or upon logging out (session cookies), others survive after your browser is closed until a defined expiration date set in the cookie (as determined by the third party placing it), and help recognize your computer when you open your browser and browse the Internet again (persistent cookies). For more details on cookies please visit All About Cookies.

Online Tracking and Do Not Track Signals: We and our third-party service providers, that may use cookies or other tracking technologies to collect information about your browsing activities over time and across different websites following your use of the Site. Our Site currently does not respond to “Do Not Track” (“DNT”) signals and operates as described in this Privacy Policy whether or not a DNT signal is received. If we do respond to DNT signals in the future, we will update this Privacy Policy to describe how we do so.

Your Choices: On most web browsers, you will find a “help” section on the toolbar. Please refer to this section for information on how to receive a notification when you are receiving a new cookie and how to turn cookies off. Please see the links below for guidance on how to modify your web browser’s settings on the most popular browsers:
